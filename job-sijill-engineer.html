SIJILL Full-time Remote Hybrid

ML / NLP
Engineer

The role

SIJILL reads documents. It answers questions. It does not require the internet.

The work this role centers on is building the pipeline that makes that happen at a real institution, on real documents, running on hardware the client already owns. Schools, law firms, government offices. Bilingual Urdu and English. Offline, always.

This is not a research role. The output is a system that runs in a room in a building. You build it, you deploy it, and you are accountable for what it does on day one at a client site.

What you will do
  • 01 Build the RAG pipeline end to end. Document ingestion, chunking, embedding, vector storage, retrieval, response generation. You own the full chain from raw file to answer. Every component is a deliberate choice.
  • 02 Work with Urdu and mixed-script documents. OCR for scanned pages, tokenization quirks in Urdu text, bilingual query handling. Pakistani institutional documents are not clean. The pipeline has to handle what actually exists.
  • 03 Run offline language models on client hardware. Local LLMs on CPU and modest GPU targets. You know how to get acceptable performance from a machine that is not a data centre.
  • 04 Ingest client documents at deployment sites. The real work happens when you sit with the client's actual files. PDFs, Word documents, scanned images, records that have never been digitised properly. You make the system work on those, not on benchmark datasets.
  • 05 Write the deployment playbook. Every deployment teaches something. You document what you learned so that the next one is faster. The first version of that playbook is part of this role's output.
What you bring
  • Python, written cleanly. Not notebooks only. Code that runs outside your machine, that someone else can read, and that does not break when the input is slightly different from what you expected.
  • LangChain or LlamaIndex, at reasonable depth. You have used one on a real problem, not just a tutorial. You understand what it is doing and where it breaks.
  • Vector databases, set up and queried. Qdrant or ChromaDB, self-hosted on a box you controlled. You know what embedding dimensions are, what a retrieval window is, and what happens when similarity scores are too flat.
  • HuggingFace, working knowledge. Loading models, running inference, using the sentence-transformers library. You have downloaded a model, run it, and looked at what it actually outputs.
  • Linux and Docker, functional. The stack does not run on a Windows laptop at a client site. You are comfortable in a terminal and you can set up a container without instructions.
  • Something built and finished. One project, academic or personal, where you took a document or text problem and built a working system. You can describe what the input was, what the output was, and what you would do differently.
The arrangement

Manar is small by design. The first hire on this side of the work is not joining a team. They are becoming the person who defines how this part of Manar deploys. That comes with more responsibility and more ownership than a normal junior role.

Base salary
PKR 80,000 to 120,000 per month depending on what you have actually built and what you demonstrate in the trial task.
Pilot bonus
A fixed bonus for every paid deployment you build and ship. PKR 20,000 to 30,000 per signed pilot. You are a stakeholder in what gets sold, not only what gets built.
Ownership of the stack
You are the named engineer on every deployment you build. The internal build playbook carries your name. As the work grows, the role grows with it.
90-day trial
At the end of three months, the bar is clear: a working demo exists and at least one pilot is in discussion. If both are true, full-time permanent offer. No vague evaluation.
How we work

Manar is small by design. Every system that gets deployed is built and reviewed directly, not by a process that obscures who did what. You will know what is being built, what it is for, and why the decisions were made the way they were.

The work happens across multiple sites, on-site at client locations when the deployment requires it, and remotely when it does not. The split depends on the deployment, not a fixed policy.

The clients are real institutions. Schools, law firms, offices that have never had anything like this before. The deployment is in their building. That is the whole point.

Apply

Send your CV and one paragraph describing the most interesting NLP or document problem you have worked on. What the input was, what the output was, and what broke.

No cover letter. No recruiter steps. The engineering team reads these directly.

Trial task
Every shortlisted candidate receives a take-home task before any conversation. A 50-page PDF. Five questions. A working RAG system that answers them. It takes four to six hours. It tells us more than any interview.
