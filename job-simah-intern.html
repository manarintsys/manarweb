SIMAH Internship 3 months On-site Lahore

Vision
Intelligence
Intern

The internship

This is a working internship. You will not be making slides. You will be learning how vision models run on real hardware, working with cameras and edge devices, and building the kind of understanding that takes most people years to get.

The hardware is here. An NVIDIA Jetson Orin Nano 8GB Super. An RTX desktop GPU. Real cameras. You will get hands-on time with all of it, with guidance from engineers who work on these systems every day.

Three months. Lahore, on-site. Strong interns are considered for full-time roles when a position is open.

Edge device
NVIDIA Jetson Orin Nano 8GB Super
The device production models run on. You will learn how to deploy inference pipelines on it.
Training hardware
NVIDIA RTX Desktop GPU
Used for training runs, fine-tuning experiments, and validation. You will run real training jobs on it.
Sensors
IP and USB cameras
Real camera inputs used to test and validate vision pipelines end to end.
Framework
PyTorch, TensorRT, OpenCV
The tools the team uses. You will learn them on real tasks, not synthetic exercises.
What you will do
  • 01 Learn edge inference end to end. Set up TensorRT pipelines, run models on the Jetson, measure the results. You will understand what makes a model fast or slow on constrained hardware.
  • 02 Work with real cameras and real data. Capture, preprocess, and feed frames into inference pipelines. This is not a dataset-download exercise.
  • 03 Run training experiments on the RTX rig. Fine-tune models, compare results, understand what the numbers mean. You will learn what changes performance and what does not.
  • 04 Support hardware-in-the-loop tests. Be present when models run against real sensors. Document what you observe. Ask questions.
  • 05 Build the habit of measuring. Before and after. Numbers. You will learn to produce evidence, not impressions.
Who this is for
  • You know Python. Well enough to write a script, read a tutorial, and debug what goes wrong. You do not need to be an expert.
  • You have done something with machine learning or computer vision. A university project, an online course with a real output, a Kaggle competition. Something you actually ran and can explain.
  • You want to work with hardware. Not just run notebooks. You are curious about what happens when a model hits a physical device and the numbers do not match expectations.
  • You ask when you do not know. This is a learning role. The expectation is not that you already know everything. It is that you are honest about what you do not know and willing to find out.
Interns who perform well are considered for the Vision Systems Engineer role when it is open.
Terms

Three months. On-site in Lahore. The internship is paid. The amount is discussed during the selection process and reflects the work involved, not a token gesture.

You will be working alongside the engineering team on real deployments. The experience is practical. If you want to learn what running vision models on edge hardware actually looks like, this is the place to do it.

Apply

Send a short message introducing yourself. Include what you have built or studied in vision or machine learning, and why working with real hardware interests you. Attach your CV.

No formal cover letter needed. Keep it direct. Tell us what you know and what you want to learn.
