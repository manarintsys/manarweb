The internship

Pakistani language is not one thing. It is Roman Urdu typed at speed on a phone. It is Urdu and English inside the same sentence. It is abbreviations, missing vowels, and words spelled six different ways depending on who is typing. Almost no AI system in the world is built to handle it properly. This internship is about changing that.

You will work on the pipeline that reads informal Pakistani text and extracts what it actually means. Before any document is searched, any action is triggered, or any answer is returned. The work is data, fine-tuning, and building the normalization layer that sits underneath everything else. It is quiet, precise, and consequential.

Three months. On-site. You will work directly alongside the founding team every day, not in a support queue, not in a separate room. The work happens together.

Base model
the base model, 3B and 7B
The model family the team works with. Strong multilingual understanding. You will learn how to fine-tune it for a specific domain.
Fine-tuning stack
HuggingFace + PEFT + unsloth
parameter-efficient fine-tuning fine-tuning on constrained hardware. You will run real training jobs, measure what changed, and understand why.
Training compute
Google Colab Pro / Kaggle
Where training experiments run. You will learn how to use cloud GPU time efficiently: start a run, monitor it, retrieve the output.
Language environment
Urdu, Roman Urdu, mixed-script
The actual language of the problem. Pakistani informal text in all its variation. This is what you will be working on every day.
What you will do
  • 01 Collect and structure training data from publicly available Pakistani text sources. Identifying where the language lives online, gathering it at scale, and making it usable as a training corpus. Not just downloading a dataset. Understanding what is in it and why it matters.
  • 02 Build preprocessing pipelines for informal, mixed-script text. Roman Urdu, code-switching, abbreviations, spelling variants, missing vowels. The language does not come clean. The pipeline has to work on what actually exists, not a sanitised version of it.
  • 03 Fine-tune language models on Pakistani domain data. Starting from the base model, using parameter-efficient fine-tuning to train efficiently on the collected corpus. Running experiments, comparing outputs, documenting what changed. Not just running the script. Understanding what the numbers mean.
  • 04 Build the semantic extraction and normalisation layer. Given an informal Pakistani query, whatever a real person typed, extract the actual meaning and produce a normalised representation the rest of the system can act on. This layer sits underneath everything else.
  • 05 Measure everything, before and after. Does the model now understand what was typed? Prove it with numbers against real examples. You will learn to produce evidence, not impressions. The output is evaluated on real informal Pakistani text, not on benchmark datasets.
Who this is for
  • You know Python. Scripts that run outside your machine, that someone else can read, that do not break when the input is slightly different from what you expected. Not just notebooks.
  • You have worked with text or language in some form. Classification, preprocessing, search, generation, anything where text was the input and something useful came out. One real project. Not a tutorial.
  • You use AI tools intelligently. This role works with AI-assisted development every day. You should be comfortable using these tools to accelerate your work, not as a replacement for understanding, but as a way to move faster when you already understand.
  • You have thought about how Pakistanis actually type. Roman Urdu, code-switching, abbreviations, the way language moves between Urdu and English in a single sentence. If you have noticed this problem and thought about it, even informally, tell us. This is the hard part. It is also the most interesting part.
  • You ask when you do not know. The work is collaborative and daily. Questions are expected. What is not acceptable is pretending to understand something you do not.
Strong interns are offered a full-time role when the internship ends. The path is direct and based entirely on what was built.
Terms

Three months. On-site. The internship is paid. The amount is discussed during the selection process and reflects the work involved.

You will be working alongside the founding team every day on a system that is actively being built. There is no support queue, no task-assignment portal. The work happens in the room, together, and you are expected to participate in it fully, not observe it.

Apply

Send your CV and a short description of one project, not a tutorial, something you built, where you worked with text or language data. Tell us what the input was, what you built, and what you would do differently now.

If you have thought specifically about Pakistani informal language, Roman Urdu, code-switching, how people type on WhatsApp or social media, mention it. What did you notice? What would you try? Applications without this are not read.
