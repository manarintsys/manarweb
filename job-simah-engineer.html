SIMAH Full-time Hybrid

Vision Systems
Engineer

The role

SIMAH reads frames. It runs on the edge. It does not call home.

The gap this role closes is the one between a model that performs in evaluation and a system that performs in production. The hardware is an NVIDIA Jetson Orin Nano 8GB Super. The job is to make SIMAH work at the throughput the deployment demands, without internet, without a cloud fallback.

You will also have access to a discrete NVIDIA GPU rig for training and validation runs. The two targets behave differently. Understanding both is the work.

What you will do
  • 01 Optimize inference on the Jetson Orin. TensorRT pipelines, INT8 and FP16 quantization, engine serialization. You choose the right approach for the target, not the default one.
  • 02 Build the camera-to-output pipeline. Frame capture, preprocessing, batching, post-processing. It needs to run continuously without frame loss. Latency is a hard requirement.
  • 03 Profile and measure. Latency, throughput, memory, power. You produce numbers before and after every change. Improvements are measured, not estimated.
  • 04 Run hardware-in-the-loop tests. The model runs against real sensors, not synthetic data. You are in the room for those tests and you understand what you are looking at.
  • 05 Support training on the GPU rig. Fine-tuning, data augmentation, evaluation pipelines. The Jetson is where it deploys. The GPU rig is where it improves.
What you bring
  • Python, solid. C++, workable. You write code that other people can read. You do not need to be an expert in both, but you need to function in both.
  • NVIDIA GPU experience. You have worked with CUDA-based tools. You know what TensorRT is and why it exists. Formal production deployment is not a requirement, but genuine hands-on time is.
  • Computer vision fundamentals. Convolutional networks, detection architectures, inference pipelines. You understand what a model is doing, not just what it outputs.
  • Something built. A project, a course, an experiment on real hardware. You have made something run on a GPU. You can show it or describe it plainly.
  • Systematic thinking. When something is slow or wrong, you find out why before you fix it. You measure, you hypothesize, you test.
How we work

Manar is small by design. The people who join are present for the full problem, not a slice of it. You will know what SIMAH is being used for, what the performance requirements are, and why they exist.

There is no team to hide behind. If you find the problem, you fix it. The hardware is here and the work is direct.

Apply

Send your CV and a short note on the most interesting GPU or vision project you have worked on. What the hardware was, what you built, and what you learned from it.

No cover letter. No recruiter steps. The engineering team reads these directly.
