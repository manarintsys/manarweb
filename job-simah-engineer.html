SIMAH Full-time On-site Lahore

Vision Systems
Engineer

The role

SIMAH reads frames. It runs on the edge. It does not call home.

The gap this role closes is the one between a model that performs in evaluation and a system that performs in production. The hardware is real. The constraints are real. The job is to make SIMAH work at the throughput and reliability that the deployment demands.

You will have two targets. An NVIDIA Jetson Orin Nano 8GB Super. And a discrete NVIDIA GPU rig for training runs and validation. They behave differently. You need to understand both.

What you will do
  • 01 Optimize inference on the Jetson Orin. TensorRT pipelines. INT8 and FP16 quantization. Engine serialization. You choose the right approach for the target performance envelope, not the default one.
  • 02 Build the camera-to-output pipeline. Frame capture. Preprocessing. Batching. Post-processing. It needs to run continuously without frame loss at the required throughput. Latency is a requirement, not a metric to observe.
  • 03 Profile everything. Latency. Throughput. Memory headroom. Power envelope on the Jetson. You produce numbers before and after every change. Improvements are measurable.
  • 04 Run hardware-in-the-loop testing. The model runs against real sensors, not synthetic data. You are present for those tests. You understand what you are seeing.
  • 05 Support training runs on the GPU rig. Fine-tuning, data augmentation, evaluation pipelines. The Jetson is where it deploys. The GPU rig is where it gets better.
  • 06 Write documentation that a second engineer can follow. The system needs to be understood by someone who was not in the room when it was built. This is not optional.
What you need
CUDA programming. Not CUDA-adjacent. You have written kernels. You know what a memory coalescing problem looks like and how to fix it.
TensorRT deployment experience. You have used it to close a real performance gap on real hardware, not to complete a tutorial.
NVIDIA Jetson hardware, hands-on. Orin preferred. Xavier acceptable. You have deployed a model that ran in a production context on Jetson hardware.
Python, proficient. C++, sufficient. The pipeline code will touch both. You can write clean, readable code in at least one.
Experience with computer vision model architectures. Convolutional networks or vision transformers. You understand the inference cost of architectural choices, not just the accuracy numbers.
Something shipped. A model that ran outside a notebook, in a real environment, with real constraints. You can describe what happened when it hit production.
Also useful
GStreamer or DeepStream pipeline construction Multi-camera synchronization and frame-accurate capture Model pruning, quantization-aware training, or distillation beyond defaults ISP tuning and camera-side image quality work Edge power management and thermal profiling on embedded hardware
How we work

Manar is small by design. The people who join are present for the full problem, not a slice of it. You will know what SIMAH is being used for, what the performance requirements are, and why they exist.

There is no team to hide behind. There is no process to wait for. If you find the problem, you fix it.

On-site in Lahore. We do not operate on remote work for this role because the hardware is here.

Apply for this role

Send your CV and one paragraph about the most constrained system you have shipped. Describe the hardware, the constraint, and what you did to close it.

No cover letter required. No recruiter calls. The engineering team reads these directly. If your background fits what we are building, you will hear back within a week.
